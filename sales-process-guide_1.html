Catalyst Marketing Sales Process Guide

Credit Repair Client Acquisition

Section 1: Sales Process
1
Call Frame & Authority
Set the tone, establish control, position as a peer
▾
Tonality: Casual, warm, unhurried
"Hey [Name], what's going on, how's the day been so far?"
Let them respond. Match their energy for 1-2 exchanges, keep it brief.
"Nice, nice. Well hey, I appreciate you jumping on. So the way I like to run these, I'm just gonna ask you some questions about your business, what's going on right now, what's working, what's not. My goal is just to understand your situation. If it makes sense for both of us, I'll walk you through how we help, and if not, no stress at all. Sound fair?"
Wait for "yeah." This is your first micro-commitment.
"Cool. So a little bit about me real quick just so you know who you're talking to. I run a credit repair business that's doing about 70k a month in net cash. Been in the credit and funding space for about 3 years. I built out my own operation, sales team, systems, all of it, and now we're helping other credit repair owners replicate what we've done. So the same principles and tactics we used to build ours is exactly what we'd be implementing for you and your business."
Tonality shift: matter-of-fact, not bragging. This is a credential drop, not a flex.
2
Discovery
Get a full snapshot of where they are right now
▾
Tonality: Genuinely curious, like a doctor asking about symptoms
"So tell me, how long have you been running your credit repair business?"
Let them answer fully. Don't rush.
"Got it. And how many active clients are you working with right now?"
"And what's revenue looking like on a monthly basis? Ballpark is fine."
"Okay cool. And how are you currently getting new clients, like what's your main source of leads right now?"
"And are you running this thing by yourself, or do you have a partner or anyone else involved in the business? Like when it comes to decisions for the business, is that all you?"
This is critical. It sets up the Spouse/Business Partner pre-handle AND the end-of-call objection handle. Whatever they say here, you'll reference later if they try to bring in an outside decision-maker.
What does the prospect say?
Referrals / Word of Mouth
"So mostly organic stuff, no real system bringing people to you consistently?"
Running Ads
"Got it, running ads yourself or you've got somebody managing that for you?"
Tried Agencies/Programs
"Interesting, we'll come back to that in a sec because I want to hear what happened there."
3
What's Not Working / Past Attempts
Expose the gap and surface failed attempts
▾
Tonality: Empathetic but direct
"So let me ask you this, how many sales calls or consultations did you book last month?"
"And of those, how many actually showed up?"
"Out of the ones that showed, how many became paying clients?"
"So what does your pipeline look like right now, are you happy with the volume of qualified people you're talking to week to week?"
They'll almost always say no. If they hedge:
"When you say it's 'okay', what does that actually look like day to day? Like are you waking up knowing you've got calls booked, or is it more like some weeks are good and some weeks it's dead?"
"How long has inconsistent lead flow been an issue for you?"
"And what have you tried to fix it so far?"
For each thing they mention, probe:
"What happened with that?"
"How long did you give that before you moved on?"
"What specifically didn't work about it?"
This is the Pain Cycle: What did you try → How long → Did it work → What else. Loop until they've exhausted their list.
"So you've tried [repeat back what they said], and none of it really moved the needle the way you needed. Is that fair to say?"
Wait for confirmation. They're now consciously aware of how many things have failed.
"Can I ask you something? After trying all of that, what do you think was actually the problem, like why do you think none of it worked?"
This gets them to self-diagnose, which sets up your pitch to fill the gap later.
What has stopped you / slowed you down from fixing your lead flow and getting more clients consistently?
I Invested / Tried X (Bad Results)
"Yeah. So here's what I see happen a lot, and tell me if this resonates. A credit repair owner knows they need more clients, so they go hire a marketing agency or buy some course or join a program. And the agency runs some ads, maybe they get some leads, but the leads are trash, or nobody's following up properly, or the whole thing falls apart after 60 days because nobody built it around YOUR business. So now you're out a few thousand bucks and you're right back where you started, except now you're more skeptical. Sound familiar?"
Wait for confirmation.
"Here's the thing though, and I'm sharing this because it matters, when we have a bad experience, it tends to cloud our judgment going forward. And we end up missing out on good opportunities because of that one bad experience. Would you agree with that?"
Wait for yes.
"So I don't want that to be the case here. What I'd rather do is just show you what we actually do, how it works, and let you decide based on THIS, not based on what some other company did or didn't do. Fair?"
Nothing / Haven't Tried
"Got it. So what slowed you down?"
Listen. Usually time, money, didn't know where to start, overwhelmed.
"So you haven't taken action on this for [however long they've had the problem]. Can I share a perspective on that?"
Wait for yes.
"Can you see how that pattern of not taking action, like not prioritizing getting a real client acquisition system in place, has put you in the exact position you're in right now? Like the inconsistent revenue, the stress of not knowing where next month's clients are coming from, that's a direct result of this not being addressed."
"Because looking back over the last [timeframe], what have you missed out on in terms of growth because this wasn't handled?"
Let them quantify the cost of their own inaction.
"So how committed are you to actually becoming the business owner who invests in themselves to get to where they want to go?"
Other Options / Another Company
"Quick question, have you already spoken with anyone else about getting more clients for your credit repair business, or are we the first conversation?"

If they have:

"Got it. When you talked to them, what did you hear that made you still open to having this conversation?"

If they haven't:

"Okay, I only ask because most credit repair owners who start looking into this end up talking to 3-4 different companies and they all hear the same buzzwords: 'We'll run your ads.' 'We'll get you leads.' 'We'll build your funnel.' But none of them actually show you the strategy behind the client acquisition or how to engineer the outcome, they just want your monthly retainer."
"And I'll be honest [Name], good luck finding another company that's going to actually build you a full system, get you results, and match you with the right strategy for YOUR business, without just prioritizing their own commission."
Deliver with a light laugh. Confident, not arrogant.
"So instead of shopping around for who sounds better, I'd rather help you get clear on what actually works. That way, even if you did talk to someone else later, you'll have the right criteria to tell who's real and who's full of it. Fair?"
Time
"When you say time has slowed you down, was it that you literally didn't have the hours, or that other priorities seemed more urgent at the moment?"
Let them answer. Almost always it's prioritization, not actual time.
"Can you see how that way of thinking, like not prioritizing building a real client acquisition system, has put you in the exact position you described earlier? Where you're [reference their pain point]?"
"Because looking back over the last [timeframe], what has it cost you in terms of lost clients, delayed growth, projects that never got off the ground, all because this wasn't addressed sooner?"
"How committed are you to becoming the business owner who prioritizes getting this handled, even if it means taking action when it's uncomfortable?"
Money
"So when you say money has slowed you down, was it that you didn't have the capital, or that you weren't confident in the return you'd get from investing?"
Listen. This separates true financial constraint from uncertainty disguised as a money objection.
"Can you see how that way of thinking around money, like holding back on investing in a real system to grow, has kept you stuck at [reference their current revenue] instead of getting to [their target]?"
"Because looking back over the last [timeframe], what have you lost out on in terms of revenue and growth because this wasn't in place?"
"How committed are you to becoming the business owner who makes strategic investments to get where they want to go, even if it means an upfront commitment?"
Spouse / Business Partner
They already told you in Discovery whether they make the decisions. Now dig deeper:
"So when it comes to business and financial decisions like this, you mentioned you run this yourself. When you do loop anyone else in, is that more about getting their opinion, or do they actually make the final decision?"
"The only reason I ask is because in my experience, the most successful business owners are the ones who take full ownership of the research and decision process themselves. They gather all the info, weigh it, then make a decision that's in the best interest of everyone involved."
"So my goal today is to make sure you have everything you need to make that call confidently, with or without anyone else's input. Sound fair?"
If they're dead set on consulting a partner before deciding, ask them to reschedule the call with the other party present. Don't pitch into a guaranteed "I need to talk to my partner" at the end.

Pre-Handle Objections
Neutralize objections before they come up
▾
Tonality: Casual, almost offhand Use what's relevant based on discovery
"Yeah, that makes sense. And honestly, that's pretty common with what I hear from most credit repair owners we talk to. Can I share something with you real quick?"
Wait for yes.
"So most people we work with have tried some version of what you just described, maybe they tried running their own ads, maybe they hired a freelancer or an agency, maybe they bought a course or joined some program that said they'd teach them marketing. And the pattern is almost always the same, either the leads were garbage, the system was too complicated to actually run while managing clients, or nobody was actually building the thing FOR them. They were just given a bunch of stuff to figure out on their own."
Pause. Let that land. They should be nodding.
"And so what ends up happening is they start thinking 'maybe paid ads just don't work for credit repair' or 'maybe I just need more referrals', when really the issue was never the strategy. It was that nobody actually built a system around THEIR business and stuck around to make sure it worked."
"The other thing I hear a lot, and tell me if this resonates, is that people feel like they need to wait until they're at a certain level before they invest in something like this. Like 'let me get to 10 clients first, then I'll worry about marketing.' But the reality is, that's the exact thinking that keeps most people stuck at the same level for years. The businesses that scale are the ones that build the acquisition system FIRST, not after. Does that make sense?"
"And look, I wouldn't be telling you this if I hadn't seen it play out hundreds of times. I'm not here to convince you of anything. I just want to make sure you have the full picture so you can make the best decision for yourself. Fair?"
5
Hell State
Highlight the cost of inaction
▾
Tonality: Slower pace, slight concern. Holding up a mirror.
"So let me ask you this, if nothing changes over the next 6 months, you keep getting the same number of leads, same inconsistent booked calls, same revenue, what does that actually do to the business?"
Let them answer. Don't fill the silence.
"And what does that look like for you personally? Like beyond the business numbers, what does that mean for your day-to-day life?"
This is where you get the real answer: stress, can't hire, can't leave their day job, relationship strain, feeling stuck.
"How long are you willing to keep operating like that?"
If they give a vague answer like "not much longer":
"What does 'not much longer' actually mean though? Like honestly, if you're in this exact same spot 12 months from now, how do you feel about that?"
"And I know you mentioned you've been dealing with this for [however long they said earlier]. So you've already given it [X months/years] of trying to figure it out on your own. At what point does the cost of NOT fixing this become bigger than the cost of fixing it?"
"Can I be real with you for a second?"
Wait for yes.
"The credit repair owners I see who stay stuck, it's rarely because they're not good at what they do. Most of you are great at actually fixing people's credit. The problem is they're spending all their time trying to figure out how to GET clients instead of actually SERVING clients. And the longer that goes on, the harder it gets, because you're burning cash, burning energy, and burning time on something that's not even your skill set. And at some point it stops feeling like a business and starts feeling like a job you can't quit."
That last line should hit. Pause after it.
6
Dream State
Build the vision of where they want to be
▾
Tonality: Optimistic, energized. Painting a picture WITH them.
"Okay so let's flip this. Let's say you had a consistent flow of qualified, booked calls coming in every single week, people who already know what credit repair is, already want help, and they're showing up ready to talk. What changes?"
Let them talk. This is their vision, not yours.
"What does that mean for you personally, beyond just the revenue?"
Probe for the deeper stuff: freedom, time with family, quitting their 9-5, hiring people, finally feeling like a real business owner.
"And what does revenue look like at that point? Like if you're closing [their close rate] of [their target call volume], where does that put you?"
Help them do the math if needed. Make the number feel real and achievable.
"So right now you're at [current revenue] and you want to be at [target revenue]. And the main thing standing between those two numbers is a consistent way to get qualified people on the phone. Is that fair?"
"What do you think is actually stopping you from getting there on your own?"
This surfaces their self-identified limiting belief AND positions your offer as the answer to whatever they say.
7
Build the Gap
Make the distance between current and dream state undeniable
▾
Tonality: Grounded, direct. Summarizing what THEY told you.
"So let me just make sure I'm hearing you right. Right now you're at about [current revenue], you've got [current client count] clients, and most of your leads are coming from [whatever they said]. You've tried [list back their failed attempts] and none of that really got you where you needed to be."
Pause. Let them confirm.
"And where you want to be is [their dream state]. And you said the main thing keeping you from getting there is [their self-identified blocker]."
"So the way I see it, you know how to fix people's credit. That's not the issue. The issue is you don't have a system that's consistently putting qualified people in front of you so you can actually do what you're good at. And every month that goes by without that system, you're leaving [do rough math] on the table. Does that feel accurate?"
"And you said you've been dealing with this for [timeframe they gave you]. So that's [X months/years] of operating below where you know you should be. At this point, do you feel like this is something you can figure out on your own, or are you at the point where you need somebody to just build the thing for you?"
This is a forced binary. Either answer leads toward your offer.
8
Commitment Check
1-10 temperature check before presenting
▾
Tonality: Relaxed, non-pressured. Temperature check, not a trap.
"Before I walk you through exactly how we'd fix this for you, on a scale of 1 to 10, how committed are you to actually solving this? 1 being 'just kicking tires' and 10 being 'I need this handled now.'"
Shut up and let them answer.
What do they say?
10 → Move to Phase 9
Proceed directly to the Transition to Presentation.
Anything Below 10
"I appreciate you being real about that. What's keeping you from being a 10?"
Whatever they say is the real objection. Handle it using the pre-handle frameworks, then re-check:
"So now that we've talked through that, where are you at now?"
Loop until they're at a 10 or you've exhausted what you can address, then proceed to Phase 9.

Bridge from discovery to pitch with zero resistance
▾
Tonality: Calm, certain. Like a doctor giving a recommendation.
"Perfect. So look, I don't feel like I have any more questions. Is there anything else you feel like I need to know about your situation that we haven't covered?"
Let them respond. Usually it's "no, I think you've got a good picture."
"Cool. Well [Name], based on everything you've told me, where you're at right now, what you've tried, where you want to go, we can definitely help. And honestly your situation is pretty similar to a lot of the credit repair owners we work with, so this isn't new territory for us."
Pause briefly. Then:
"So where do you want to go from here? I can walk you through exactly how we'd build this out for you, the whole system, A to Z, if you want to see what it looks like."
They'll say yes. This is them ASKING you to pitch. You didn't push into it.
"Alright cool. So what I'm going to do is share my screen and walk you through exactly what we build, how it works, and what it looks like in action. Rep FAQ — Know This Before You Present
▶ "What is GHL / Go High Level?"
The all-in-one software that runs the client's funnel, automations, calendar booking, and CRM. Think of it as the command center for their entire client acquisition system. You don't need to explain the software in depth. Just show what it does for them.
▶ "What's a funnel?"
A single web page designed to get one action. In our case, booking a call. "It's a page that turns strangers into booked appointments" is all you need to say.
▶ "What are automations?"
Pre-built text, email, and voicemail sequences that fire automatically when a lead takes an action or doesn't. Think of it like a virtual assistant that never sleeps and never forgets to follow up.
▶ "What does 'cost per result' mean in Ads Manager?"
How much we're paying per lead. If cost per result is $10, every person who submits their info costs $10 in ad spend. What matters more is cost per booked call and cost per closed client.
▶ "What's a realistic daily budget?"
$50-$100/day is the sweet spot for credit repair. That's $1,500-$3,000/month in ad spend. At an average cost per lead of $5-$10, that's a significant number of leads per month flowing into their system.
▶ "What if the prospect asks about ad spend on top of our fee?"
Ad spend is separate from our build fee. Frame it as: "The investment with us is to build the machine. Ad spend is the fuel that runs it. You control the budget. We recommend $50-$100 a day to start, and you can scale up as revenue grows."
▶ "What if I don't know the answer to a technical question?"
Say: "That's a great question. I want to make sure I give you the right answer on that, so let me get that specific detail for you after the call. But what I can tell you is [redirect to outcome/result]." Never guess. Never bullshit.

Section 4: End of Call Objection Handling

⚠ Universal Opener — Run This First On Every Objection

"Got it, totally understand. Just to make sure I understand, do you feel like what I shared with you can solve the problem you have?"
Wait for answer.
"Got it. What makes you feel that way?"
Let them explain. Then:
"Okay got it. So since you're solid on the solution, it sounds like there's maybe something internal or maybe something financial that's coming up for you. If you could maybe be transparent with me and let me know what that is, maybe there's a way I can help."
Now they'll give you the REAL objection. Whatever they say determines which branch below.
Objection
"I Need to Think About It"
▾
Tonality: Calm, not confrontational. Genuinely trying to understand.
"Totally get it, [name]. And I respect that. Can I ask you something though? When you say you need to think about it, what specifically are you thinking about? Is it more about the process and how it works, whether the outcome is realistic for you, or is it more about the numbers and cost?"
This forces them to categorize their hesitation into one of three buckets.
Where do they land?
A) The Process (How It Works)
Go back to the pitch/presentation. Summarize the key points. Then:
"Does that cover what you needed to think through?"
If yes, re-close. If no, ask what's still unclear.
B) The Outcome (Will It Work For Me)
"Can I share a perspective? In life, success, business, there's no guarantees. The only guarantee in life besides death and taxes is that nothing changes if nothing changes. The only thing we can do as humans is put ourselves in the best position possible to get the outcomes we want. Right?"
"There's no guarantee if I go to the gym I'll lose weight. But does it put me in a better position than staying at home? Absolutely."
"Do we guarantee our system will get you [specific result]? No. But does it put you in a far better position than staying where you are right now with [reference their current pain]? Yes."
"All it comes down to is what's riskier. Is it riskier for us to build you this system and put you in a position to actually grow? Or is it riskier that you don't do anything, continue sitting in the same spot, and nothing changes? Which one do you feel is riskier?"
Wait for answer.
"Why?"
Wait.
"Because what do you feel happens if you keep making the same decision and things don't change for another 2 weeks, 2 months, 2 years?"
"So what do we have to do right now to put you in the best position possible to start getting results?"
C) The Numbers / Cost
Route to the Money objection handle below.
Objection
"I Don't Have the Money" / "It's Too Expensive"
▾
Tonality: Calm, zero judgment. Problem-solving with them.
"Got it. So can I ask, if money weren't in the equation would you move forward with getting this set up?"
Wait for answer. If yes, continue. If they hesitate, there's a hidden objection. Go back to universal opener.
"Why would you do it though?"
Let them re-sell themselves on why they need this. Then:
"Okay so with the money being the thing, what's the reason we're on this call right here? It's cause you want to get more clients, grow your revenue, and stop leaving money on the table, right? So is the problem that you don't have money, or that you don't have the leverage or the structure to GET money?"
Let them answer.
"Exactly. Because what happens if you allow the lack of money to stop you from getting the leverage and structure you need so that you're never in this position again? What happens then?"
Let them sit with that.
"Now I can help you with the investment. I can't help you with the commitment to want to get better. Now is that something you are committed to?"
Wait for yes.
"But why now? Why not just push this off another few months?"
Let them tell you why now matters. They're closing themselves.
"So just be real with me. Is it that we have to figure out the logistics of making this work financially, or is it that it's just scary and you're looking for a reason to stay comfortable?"
